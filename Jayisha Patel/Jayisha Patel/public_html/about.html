ABOUT

Jayisha Patel is an award winning filmmaker who works at the intersection of documentary film and virtual reality. Her works have premiered at festivals such as Berlin Film Festival, Sundance and New York Film Festival amongst others.

Her works embody an intersectional female gaze and seek to give a platform to stories about women of color fighting stereotypes, in bold and unconventional ways.

Her short film, A Paradise was nominated for over 36 international awards and was promoted by the British Council as ‘exemplary British filmmaking’ whilst her film, India’s Wushu Warriors for Al Jazeera’s Witness strand amassed over 9 million views when broadcast. It was recently a SIMA Impact finalist and was awarded the Jury’s Special Mention. Her short film, Circle also premiered at the Berlinale and was nominated for a student BAFTA.

In 2017 she directed Notes To My Father, an award winning VR experience commissioned by Oculus from Facebook’s VR for Good creative lab. The film launched during the Sundance Film Festival, before premiering at SXSW. It was produced by Golden Globe nominated studio, Flight Studio and executive produced by Gabo Arora. Notes To My Father was cited by Vice as ‘groundbreaking’ and by Documentary magazine as ‘giving a fresh perspective to the cinematic language of point of view’ The film won several awards including the UN Woman’s Global Voices Award for Best 360 film and the Audience Award at Dok Leipzig. It was featured at Tedx Hyderabad and presented to policy advisors at the World Economic Forum’s India Summit.

She was recently featured in a Guardian article about women further the future of storytelling.

She is currently an artist in residence at Somerset House, where she is working on her latest multi-sensory immersive experience, After the Fire and the animated VR experience, HOME which is commissioned by Greenpeace.

She has presented and spoken about her work on the likes of BBC Radio 4’s Woman’s Hour , the United Nations, the V and A and at the British Film Institute. Aside from her artistic practice, she lectures in immersive storytelling at University College London (UCL).

She studied documentary direction at the International Film and TV School, Cuba and at the National Film and TV School, UK.
